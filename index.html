Analysis of Monoterpene Fragrances' Effects on the Proliferation of Staphyloccocus epidermidis

Investigating Common Skincare Fragrances

Background

The skincare industry has seen unprecedented growth is the past few years, as companies tap into the personal care product (PCP) market. Many of these PCPs claim to be beneficial for skin health, but loose FDA regulations mean that the effects of the products aren't truly known. This study focuses on a specific sub-class of chemicals commonly used in skincare products: monoterpene fragrances. They are naturally derived from plants and are used in low concentrations in many facial moisturizers.

Rationale

Monoterpene fragrances are classified as volatile organic compounds (VOCs), and their impact on the facial microbiome is poorly understood. This study specifically looks at the bacteria Staphyloccocus epidermidis, a common facial bacteria known for activating immune response against viruses and preserving facial moisture. The effect the fragrances have on S. epidermidis will help predict their overall impact on skin health, and whether PCPs that contain these compounds are having a negative effect on skin health.
